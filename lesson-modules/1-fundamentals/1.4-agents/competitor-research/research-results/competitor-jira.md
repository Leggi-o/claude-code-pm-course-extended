# Competitor Analysis: Jira

## Features
- Scrum and Kanban boards
- Agile reporting and custom workflows
- Cross-team planning (Premium+)
- Custom fields, task dependencies
- Customizable approval processes
- Automation rules (100 free, unlimited on paid)
- Deep Atlassian ecosystem (Confluence, Bitbucket)

## Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 10 users, 2GB storage
- **Standard:** $9.05/user/month (up to 100 users)
- **Premium:** $18.30/user/month (up to 100 users)
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

## Positioning
- Enterprise issue tracking and agile project management
- Deeply embedded in software development workflows
- Part of the Atlassian ecosystem play

## Strengths
- Industry standard for software development teams
- Extremely powerful customization and workflows
- Deep integration with development tools
- Massive marketplace of plugins and add-ons

## Weaknesses
- Steep learning curve, complex administration
- Primarily engineering-focused, poor for non-technical teams
- UI feels dated compared to modern competitors
- Can become expensive with required add-ons

## Opportunities for TaskFlow
- Target cross-functional teams frustrated by Jira's engineering-only focus
- Modern, clean UI vs Jira's dated interface
- Simpler setup and administration for growing teams
