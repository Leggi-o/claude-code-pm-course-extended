# Competitor Analysis: Asana

## Features
- Task management with multiple views (list, board, timeline, Gantt)
- Workflow automation builder (unlimited on paid plans)
- Goals tracking (individual, team, org-wide)
- Portfolio views and workload management
- Custom fields and forms
- AI Teammates (agentic AI for task collaboration)
- Integrations with Salesforce, Power BI, Tableau

## Pricing
- **Personal (Free):** Up to 10 users, unlimited tasks/projects
- **Starter:** $10.99/user/month (billed annually)
- **Advanced:** $24.99/user/month (billed annually)
- **Enterprise / Enterprise+:** Custom pricing

## Positioning
- Enterprise project management leader targeting mid-market to large organizations
- "Work management platform for teams of all sizes"
- Strong focus on cross-functional collaboration and AI-powered workflows

## Strengths
- Brand recognition and market maturity
- Comprehensive feature set across all PM needs
- Strong enterprise security and compliance
- Aggressive AI investment (AI Teammates, smart workflows)

## Weaknesses
- Expensive at scale ($24.99/user for advanced features)
- Complexity overwhelms smaller teams
- Poor mobile experience (common user complaint)
- Slow performance with large datasets

## Opportunities for TaskFlow
- Win on price: TaskFlow Pro at $12/user vs Asana Advanced at $24.99/user
- Target teams frustrated by Asana's complexity with our simpler, async-first approach
- Better mobile experience as a key differentiator
